Haz vs Has

Haz comes from hacer and is used to give commands.

✅ Haz tu tarea.

Has comes from haber and is used with another verb.

✅ Has estudiado mucho.

Ser vs Estar

Ser is used for permanent things.

• Name
• Nationality
• Profession

Estar is used for temporary things.

• Feelings
• Location
• Current condition
Hay · Ahí · Ay

Hay = there is / there are

✅ Hay un gato.

Ahí = there (location)

✅ Está ahí.

Ay = pain or surprise

✅ ¡Ay! Me duele.

The 4 Porqués

¿Por qué? = used in questions.

✅ ¿Por qué estudias español?

Porque = used in answers.

✅ Porque me gusta.

Porqué = the reason.

✅ No entiendo el porqué.

Por que = uncommon expression used in specific sentences.

Looks Familiar?

Some Spanish words look like English...

But they mean something different!

❌ Embarazada = Embarrassed

✅ Embarazada = Pregnant

❌ Carpeta = Carpet

✅ Carpeta = Folder

❌ Éxito = Exit

✅ Éxito = Success

Sí vs Si

Sí = yes

✅ Sí, claro.

Si = if

✅ Si estudias, aprenderás.

Mucho vs Muy

Mucho is used with nouns (things).

✅ Mucho trabajo.
A lot of work.

Muy is used with adjectives (descriptions).

✅ Muy bonito.
Very pretty.

Silent H

The letter H is never pronounced in Spanish.

✅ Hola

✅ Hora

✅ Hambre
